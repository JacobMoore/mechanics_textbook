Conservation of Momentum in Rigid Bodies and Systems of Rigid Bodies

When there are no linear impulses acting on a rigid body, we can say that linear momentum is conserved for that body. When there are no angular impulses acting on a rigid body about a particular point, we can say that angular momentum is conserved for the body about that point. That is, the momentum is the same between states, or between initial and final times. Impulses are external forces or moments that act over time.

Conservation of linear momentum: \[\vec{J_1} = \vec{J_2} \]
\[ m \vec{v_{i}} = m \vec{v_{f}} \]
Conservation of angular momentum: \[\vec{K}_{G,1} = \vec{K}_{G,2} \]
\[ I_G \vec{\omega_{i}} = I_G \vec{\omega_{f}}\]

Recall that there are additional expressions of angular momentum about other points.

For an object, one direction of type of momentum may be conserved while others are not.

Conservation of momentum for a system of rigid bodies

While conservation of momentum for a single rigid body can be useful, it is more powerful when considering systems of rigid bodies. By considering two or more objects as part of the system, any forces or moments acting between those objects (i.e. contact forces, impact forces, etc.) are now internal to the system. Since we only consider external forces and moments when determining impulse, these forces no longer contribute to the impulse side of our impulse-momentum equation. This can allow us to use the simpler conservation of momentum equations, rather than performing integrals on complex time-varying forces, such as impact forces.

Conservation of linear momentum across a system of rigid bodies: \[\sum_{sys} \vec{J_1} = \sum_{sys} \vec{J_2} \]
\[ \vec{J_{1,1}} + \vec{J_{1,2}} + \ldots + \vec{J_{1,n}} = \vec{J_{2,1}} + \vec{J_{2,2}} + \ldots + \vec{J_{2,n}} \]
Conservation of angular momentum across a system of rigid bodies: \[\sum_{sys} \vec{K_{G,1}} = \sum_{sys} \vec{K_{G,2}} \]
\[ \vec{K_{G,1,1}} + \vec{K_{G,1,2}} + \ldots + \vec{K_{G,1,n}} = \vec{K_{G,2,1}} + \vec{K_{G,2,1}} + \ldots + \vec{K_{G,2,n}} \]

Note that the specific linear or angular momentum of one body in the system does not necessarily stay the same between states (i.e. momentum is not necessarily conserved for any one body in the system).

Conservation of angular momentum also works for momentum about points other than G, such as a pin, an ICZV, or any point P. Often, one can select a point about which the forces external to a system have zero-length moment arms, leading to conservation of angular momentum for the system about that point (even when angular momentum of the system is not conserved about other points, and/or linear momentum of the system is not conserved).
